# The Universe — A Journey Through Space

An educational website exploring the wonders of the cosmos—from galaxies and stars to black holes—while examining how philosophical thinking shaped our understanding of the universe.

## About This Project

This website was created as an informational resource about space, astronomy, and the human quest to understand our cosmic origins. It covers topics ranging from the Big Bang to the nature of black holes, while highlighting the philosophical foundations that drove many scientific discoveries.

## Contents

- **The Universe** — The Big Bang, dark matter/energy, composition of cosmos
- **Galaxies** — Types of galaxies, the Milky Way structure, galactic interactions
- **Earth** — How our planet works (core, mantle, crust, systems)
- **Stars** — Lifecycle, element creation, different star types
- **Black Holes** — Formation, event horizons, singularities
- **Philosophy & Science** — How philosophical thinking shaped discoveries from ancient Greeks to Einstein

## Key Topics Covered

### The Universe
- Age of the universe (13.8 billion years)
- Dark matter (27%) and dark energy (68%)
- 2 trillion galaxies with ~200 billion trillion stars

### Earth Systems
- Core, mantle, and crust structure
- Water, carbon, and atmospheric cycles
- Magnetic field protection

### Star Lifecycle
- Nebulae → Main Sequence → Red Giant → Supernova/Collapse
- Element creation through nuclear fusion
- Connection to human existence ("we are made of star stuff")

### Black Holes
- Stellar mass and supermassive varieties
- Event horizons and singularities
- First images from Event Horizon Telescope (2019, 2022)

### Philosophy in Science
- Greek foundations (Aristotle, Ptolemy)
- Copernican Revolution
- Newton's universal laws
- Einstein's philosophical influences
- Modern questions about reality and existence
